Abstract:
Overview: Agriculture plays a vital role in the economy, but farmers often struggle to select suitable crops due to varying soil and environmental conditions, leading to reduced productivity and financial loss.
Proposed Framework: This project proposes a Machine Learning-based Decision Support System for smart farming that recommends optimal crops and fertilizers based on soil nutrients and climatic factors.
Key Method: A Random Forest algorithm is used to analyze parameters such as Nitrogen (N), Phosphorus (P), Potassium (K), temperature, humidity, pH, and rainfall to predict the most suitable crop.
Results: Experimental results show that the model achieves high prediction accuracy (around 90%) and provides reliable recommendations for different environmental conditions.
Impact: The system helps farmers make informed decisions, improves crop yield, reduces resource wastage, and supports sustainable agriculture.

Introduction:
Background: Agriculture is the backbone of many economies, and technological advancements have significantly improved farming practices through automation and data analysis.
Current Limitation: Traditional farming relies heavily on farmer experience and lacks scientific decision-making tools to analyze soil and environmental data.
Need: Improper crop selection due to lack of knowledge about soil nutrients and weather conditions results in low productivity and economic loss.
Research Gap: There is a need for a simple, user-friendly system that integrates machine learning techniques for crop and fertilizer recommendation.
Objective: To develop a Smart Farming Decision Support System that provides accurate crop and fertilizer recommendations using machine learning.

Problem Statement:
Core Problem: Farmers lack proper guidance in selecting suitable crops based on soil and environmental conditions.
Impact on Farmers: 
Low crop yield 
Financial loss 
Inefficient use of fertilizers 
Environmental Issues:
Overuse of fertilizers damages soil quality
Poor irrigation planning affects crop growth

Proposed Solution:
Solution Overview: A Smart Farming Decision Support System that uses machine learning to recommend crops and fertilizers.
ML Model: A Random Forest classifier is used to predict the most suitable crop based on input features.
Fertilizer Recommendation: A rule-based approach is used to suggest fertilizers based on nutrient deficiency.
System output:
 Recommended crop 
 Suggested fertilizer 
Integration: The system is implemented as a simple web application using Flask for user interaction.

Methodology:
Dataset: Crop recommendation dataset containing soil and environmental parameters.
Step 1 – Data Collection:Collect dataset with features like N, P, K, temperature, humidity, pH, and rainfall.
Step 2 – Data Preprocessing:Clean data, handle missing values, and split into training and testing sets.
Step 3 – Model Training:Train Random Forest model using training data.
Step 4 – Prediction:Model predicts crop based on user input.
Step 5 – Fertilizer Suggestion:Rule-based logic provides fertilizer recommendation.

Challenges:
Data Quality: Inaccurate or incomplete data can affect prediction accuracy.
Input Dependency: Incorrect user input may lead to wrong recommendations.
Environmental Variability: Weather conditions change frequently and may affect results.
Limited Scope: System is limited to predefined dataset and conditions.

Outcome / Results:
Model Performance:Random Forest model achieved approximately 90% accuracy in predicting suitable crops.
System Output:Provides accurate crop and fertilizer recommendations based on user input.
Efficiency:Helps in faster decision-making compared to traditional methods.
Impact:Improves productivity and reduces resource wastage.
